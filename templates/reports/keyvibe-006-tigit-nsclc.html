The structural argument

By the time KeyVibe-006 was designed, anti-TIGIT antibodies had accumulated a clean string of Phase 3 failures. SKYSCRAPER-01 (tiragolumab in NSCLC), SKYSCRAPER-02 (tiragolumab in SCLC), and AdvanTIG-302 (ociperlimab in 1L NSCLC, NCT04746924) had all missed. The mechanism class had a zero percent Phase 3 success rate across multiple cancer types and sponsors.

The structural reason is that TIGIT engages T cells at the same effector encounter step that PD-1 already addresses. Adding TIGIT to PD-1 does not extend coverage to a new step in the immune cycle — it stacks a second inhibitor on a step already covered. The framework predicts that regimens which stack at one step rather than covering additional independent steps produce marginal or no additional benefit, and KeyVibe-006 was a textbook example of that design.

Consolidation after concurrent chemoradiation in Stage III NSCLC is already held by durvalumab as standard of care (PACIFIC). Replacing durvalumab with a PD-1 + TIGIT doublet, where the TIGIT component had no Phase 3 precedent of success, was structurally identical to the seven trials that had already failed.

They are included because the structural argument is the same either way, and the direction match matters for the track record.

The backtest: if KeyVibe-006 had crossed its PFS threshold, the framework's structural claim about TIGIT + PD-1 stacking would have been refuted, and the class would have a non-zero Phase 3 success rate for the first time. The trial was discontinued for futility on December 16, 2024.
