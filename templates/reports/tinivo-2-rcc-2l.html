The structural argument

TiNivo-2 asked whether continuing PD-1 blockade alongside a VEGFR TKI, in patients who had already progressed on a prior ICI, could extend PFS beyond the TKI alone. The design implicitly assumed that PD-1 blockade retains structural value after the tumour has demonstrated escape from it. The framework's position is that once a tumour has escaped PD-1-mediated immune pressure, re-introducing PD-1 blockade in a new combination does not restore the lost surveillance — the immune-cycle step the drug addresses is the same step the tumour has already adapted around.

The control arm was tivozanib alone. Tivozanib is an active VEGFR TKI and has its own mechanism-of-action — anti-angiogenic — that is not about immune engagement. The experimental arm added nivolumab on top of tivozanib, reintroducing PD-1 blockade into a setting where the tumour had previously escaped from exactly that inhibition. The framework predicts that adding a drug targeting an already-escaped step produces no meaningful additive benefit over the active comparator.

The same structural pattern had been observed in prior post-ICI rechallenge studies in other indications, and TiNivo-2 was the RCC version of that pattern. The framework places it in the same class of designs that add an already-escaped mechanism to an active backbone and expect synergy.

What the framework would have predicted

Each row below is the call the framework makes from the trial design alone, without any knowledge of the readout. This is a backtest — the readout was already public when the entry was added to the scorecard — so these rows are honest retrospective claims, not prospective ones. They are included because the structural argument is the same either way, and the direction match matters for the track record.

The backtest: if TiNivo-2 had shown a PFS advantage for the nivolumab-containing arm, the framework's claim that re-engaging an already-escaped immune step adds nothing to an active non-immune backbone would have been falsified. AVEO reported the trial missed its primary PFS endpoint at ESMO 2024 in September.
